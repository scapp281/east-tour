Thousand Island Introduction

Thousand Island are an island group that straddles between New York's North Country and Eastern Ontario, where Lake Ontario and St. Lawrence River meets. During the 18th and most of the 19th century, it was a choke point on the St. Lawrence and therefore of vital military importance for both the young United States and the British colony of Canada. Now, it is a popular cottage country to spend July and August holidays.

At the 20th century, the Thousand Islands were mainly a retreat for the wealthy. Some of them bought a private island for their summer masonry castles. These are visible on boat cruises down the river. Wealthy and middle-class summer residents built summer homes. Some masonry "castles" remain as international landmarks. The most famous extant examples are "The Towers" on Dark Island, now called Singer Castle, and the long-neglected Boldt Castle on Heart Island. Boldt Castle, a 120-room mansion, is a testament of George Boldt's love to his wife. Boldt Castle and surrounding grounds are available for touring, and is also available for wedding ceremonies.

It is a very popular destination for beautiful scenery in summer. Thousand Island is comprised of approximate 1700 islands. The smallest of the Thousand Islands supports only one tree and two shrubs. The largest of the Thousand Islands is Wolfe Island, which is an entrance to the St. Lawrence River in Lake Ontario near Kingston, Ontario. The water is so clear in some areas, that a rocky bottom under 80 feet of water can be seen. Around 20 of these islands form the Thousand Islands National Park, the oldest of Canada's national parks. The Thousand Islands Bridge connects New York State and Ontario, one of the ten provinces of Canada, traversing Interstate 81 and Highway 137.

In addition to Thousand Islands' scenic view, Thousand Islands is popular location for boating, fishing and vacationing. One of the few beaches in the Thousand Islands, Potter's Beach on the American Grindstone Island has a fine, shallow sandy bottom with a very gradual slope, provide a perfect location to anchor up and spend a day at the beach either swimming or hiking on the trails that extend around Grindstone Island. A popular location for swimming or anchoring out of the wind, the Lake of the Isles is a secluded area cut off from the rest of the St. Lawrence River by Wellesley Island and neighboring Canadian Hill Island. Known for its fishing, Eel Bay is a shallow bay just southwest of Wellesley Island.
