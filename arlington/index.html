Arlington Introduction

Arlington is an urban county in Virginia located directly across the Potomac River from Washington DC. Despite its densely population, Arlington balances environments of both big city and suburban. Arlington has a lot of amenities, low crime rates, and high income per Capita. Arlington is an incredibly safe and peaceful area to raise children.

Many tourists visit Washington DC as part of a trip to Arlington. Usually takes 14 minutes to travel between these two cities by train. Most tourists choose to visit D.C. during the summer. Between November and March, there are fewer visitors and thus lower hotel rates.

The busiest times of the time is on April, as the Cherry Blossom blooming along the Tidal Basin. Tourists come from all over the world to see the spectacular view. Donated by Japan as a gesture of friendship and peace, the Cherry Blossom trees in DC have graced the Tidal Basin and Potomac Waterfront for over 100 years.

Arlington is home of national memorials and museums, including Arlington National Cemetery, the Pentagon Memorial, the Marine Corps War Memorial, and the United States Air Force Memorial. The Arlington Cemetery is a must see if you are in Virginia or DC. Arlington Cemetery, established during the Civil War, serves as a cemetery and a memorial to America's persons of national importance, including presidents, Supreme Court justices and countless heroic soldiers and marines. More than three million people visit Arlington annually, attending graveside services, which are conducted Monday to Saturday, and special ceremonies to pay final respects to presidents, veterans and historical figures. The Iwo Jima Memorial is also located nearby, and is also a must see. More surprisingly, if you walk around the memorial counter clockwise, you will notice the optical illusion of the marines planting the flag. This is a good spot for an excellent view of the fireworks during July 4th. Mystery Dinner Playhouse is one of the great places in Arlington, VA for dinner while seeing the play.
