Montreal Introduction

Montreal is one of three largest city in Quebec. This huge city is divided in several districts. It is well-deserved reputation as one of the liveliest cities. Montreal is the second largest French- city in the world, behind Paris. Montreal is the cultural and economic center in Quebec. During the 1920s and 1930s, there is a prohibition on manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcohol in the United States. During this time, Montreal became a mecca for cross-border fun seekers from nearby New England and New York.

Montreal itself is a very safe, open, and inviting city. Montreal is an extremely inviting destination for gay and lesbian tourists. Canada's contributions to gay rights have recently become widely known, but Quebec was the first province in Canada to pass a non-discrimination law for sexual orientation and to provide same-sex civil unions. Same-sex marriage is legal in Quebec was well as in the rest of Canada.

In the districts of Old Montreal, there are many historical buildings from the 17th to 19th century and many museums. At night, some of these buildings are beautifully lit up. Shopaholics will find much to love here. All the big labels can be found on the city's popular street, Saint Catherine Street, which is called rue Ste-Catherine in French. To experience French lifestyle, try the alluring shops and eateries along mainly French Saint Denis street. Finally, stroll along the Saint Paul Street, which is the Montreal's oldest street that paved with cobblestones.

Once known as the city of 100 churches, Montréal is now the city of 100 festivals. Montreal has a bewildering variety of festivals. These music, film, comdey, and other kinds, festivals range from one day to huge international productions running two weeks or more. They are generally held in the summer and autumn. Just For Laughs, Shakespeare in the Park, Festival Mondiale de la bière, Fantasia, Cinémania, Francofolies, just to name a few. Shakespeare in the Park is one of the interesting one, where Repercussion Theatre puts on outdoor performances of Shakespeare plays free of charge. This summer come watch.
