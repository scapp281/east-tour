Plymouth Introduction

It makes a good stop between Boston and Cape Cod. Plymouth holds a place of great prominence in American history. Everyone educated in United States knows at least a little of the story about Pilgrims, seeking a place where they could practice their religion as they wished, without interference from government, left Europe on the Mayflower and land at Plymouth Rock, an innocuous, weathered granite ball, in the winter of 1620. Now, Plymouth is known as America's Home Town. Plymouth was the site of the colony founded and where New England was first established. It was here that the first English colonists made war and then peace with the Natives. This place has served as the location of the prominent and notable event, the First Thanksgiving feast.

September and October can be excellent times to visit. Marks as a first stop of American history, many museums and historic houses in the surrounding streets help visitors recall Pilgrims struggles, sacrifices and triumphs. Plimoth Plantation Museum is one of those museums. Travel back in time to see how life was for the Pilgrims and Wampanoag American Indians in the 1600s by interact with costumed actors in the museum. These actor who reenact the Pilgrims' daily tasks in the English colony. At the Wampanoag home site that has been recreated alongside the colony, you’ll meet American Indians from different nations who demonstrate how the Wampanoag's ancestors lived and talk about how they interacted with the pilgrims. They also tell you about how they live in present day and will answer questions.

What you won't know how dangerous life was at the time until you board the Mayflower II, a replica of the original ship that the pilgrims sailed to Plymouth. Historical actors describe the journey and living conditions aboard the Mayflower during the Atlantic crossing. Bringing history to life with vivid detail as you walk about the ship.

It's no wonder that the Pilgrims fell in love with America when they landed at gorgeous Plymouth with calm waters of Plymouth Harbor along Cape Cod Bay. Most historic attractions are both educational and fun. It is enjoyable if you're traveling with children. Visit the landmarks that honor this piece of American history, from Plymouth Rock to Mayflower II to Burial Hill. Choose to climb Cole’s Hill, a National Historic Landmark, which contains the first cemetery used by the pilgrims. Or just enjoy the sandy beaches and calm sea breeze.
