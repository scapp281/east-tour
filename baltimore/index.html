Baltimore Introduction

As always, Baltimoreans are happy to see new faces. If you hear an old Baltimorean favorite greeting "Hi, Hon!" you can be sure you're welcome. Baltimore is the largest city in the state of Maryland and the 26th largest city in the country. It is located in the central area of the state along the tidal portion of the Patapsco River, an arm of the Chesapeake Bay. Baltimore is comprised of more than 225 distinct and unique neighborhoods. The city has been called as "a city of neighborhoods" by Baltimoreans and is nicknamed Charm City.

Baltimore was founded in 1729 as a port and shipbuilding town. Baltimore clippers were once cutting-edge technology here. Now the city provides top-notch healthcare, high-tech industries, and nonprofits. Baltimore's Inner Harbor was once the second leading port of entry for immigrants to the United States and a major manufacturing center. After a decline in manufacturing, Baltimore shifted to a service-oriented economy, with the Johns Hopkins Hospital and Johns Hopkins University serving as the city's top two employers.

The tracks for the nation's first railroad were laid there in 1829.The city's role in War of 1812 and Francis Scott Key's writing of "The Star-Spangled Banner" contributed to the city's historical importance. Baltimore's history and attractions have allowed the city to become a top tourist destination on the East Coast. In 2012, 21.3 million visitors visit downtown Baltimore alone, spending $4.4 billion.

Baltimore likes to celebrate. During May, thousands of people visit old-fashioned Flower Mart, where artists, arts and crafts vendors, schools, wellness vendors and non-profit vendors conducted their business. Linganore wines were all served in the Food Court. Food vendors sold handmade chocolate bonbons, kettle corn, strudel, nuts, and ice cream. There were decorating, art, costume, and dance contests. In midsummer, there are parties in Harborplace. Baltimoreans always like join together and celebrate. No exception in wintry night. Head east to Power Plant Live, premier dining and entertainment destination at downtown, and you'll find hot crowds and cool music. Its downtown business district has been transformed into a mecca of sparkling new hotels, retail centers, and office buildings.
