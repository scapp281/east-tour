New York Introduction

New York City was the capital of the United States in the 1780s. Known as "The Empire State". Unlike most subways around the world, the New York subway is open 24 hours a day. No doubt why it has another name called "City That Never Sleeps". A major centre for media, culture, fashion, food, shopping and finance and trade. With a towering skyline, endless supply of restaurants, entertainment, and over 4000 street food vendors, considered by many other to be the greatest city for vacation.

While in Manhattan, there are three must see attractions in New York-- Empire State Building, Times Square and Carnegie Hall. Empire State Building is the tallest building in the world for 40 years. Times Square is located at the junction of Broadway and Seventh Avenue, which is known as the Crossroads of the World and stretching from West 42nd to West 47th Streets. As a major commercial intersection and a neighborhood, Times Square surrounds you with ginormous, electronic billboards, which is just breath-taking. Carnegie Hall is one of the most prestigious concert venue where the world's most gifted musicians come to perform both classical music and popular music in about 250 performances each season.

Another top New York attraction is the beloved Statue of Liberty. You may not know that this colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in the middle of New York Harbor was French Government's gift for America's 100th anniversary of independence. Lady Liberty became America's icon for freedom pursuit.
