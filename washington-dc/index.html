Washington DC Introduction

As a nation's capital , Washington DC is known worldwide as administrative, political, economic, legislative, and judicial center of the United States. But it is so much more. Its landmarks, historic sites, diversity of neighborhoods, and business districts helps Washington DC becomes a major tourist attraction and mecca for commercials, documentaries, music video, television, and film. Many best films such as Breach, Wedding Crashers, No End in Sight, The Biggest Loser, The Contender, GI Joe, The Real Housewives of DC, Shooter, Where in the World is Osama Bin Laden, and Bones. It is not a surprise that Washington is home to film festivals each year and screening at museums, embassies, and cultural centers.

First thought that comes to mind when it comes to visit the District is the landmark, historic home and office of the United States president. Famous national landmarks included World War II Memorial, Washington Monuments, Capitol Building, Octagon House and National Mall. For animals lovers, National Zoo is a must go place. National Zoo has over 2000 individual animals, plus Mei Xiang and Tian Tian, their two giant pandas.

The World War II Memorial is opened to the public on 2004 on 17th Street between Constitution and Independence Avenues. The memorial is a monument to commemorate the 16 million soldiers who served in the US armed forces, including the 400,000 soldiers who died in the fight against fascism, and all who supported the war effort from home. The memorial is open to visitors 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

National Mall is where history happens. Located in the center Washington D.C. This is where Martin Lither King, Jr. delivered his "I Have A Dream" speech in front of The Lincoln Memorial This is where protestors and marchers make their voices heard. The National Mall is home to famous monuments and memorials including Martin Luther King, Lincoln, Jefferson, Franklin Delano Roosevelt, and Vietnam Veterans Memorial Wall. You will also find the National World War II Memorial and the Korean War Veterans Memorial. There are many popular museums along the National Mall such as National Museum of the American Indian, National Museum of Natural History, National Air and Space Museum, National Museum of American History, and the Smithsonian Castle.

Washington Monument is a obelisk 555 feet tall building designed by Robert Mills to honor the first President George Washington. This monument is the tallest building in the District of Columbia. By law, other buildings in D.C. are not allowed to be taller. At the top of the monument, there are a lightning rod and a nine inches tall pyramid made of aluminum, which was very expensive when the monument was first began to build in 1848.

Octagon House, is known as Colonel John Tayloe's home and one of the oldest buildings. Octagon House is a three-story brick house designed by William Thornton, who is the first architect of the United States Capitol. Colonel John Tayloe offered the use of his home to President and Mrs. Madison for a temporary "Executive Mansion" after the burning of the White House by the British. Treaty of Ghent was signed there to end the War of 1812.
