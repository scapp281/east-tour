Portland Introduction

Portland , the largest city in Maine, is known as The Forest City. Portland is just 106 miles north from Boston. Tourists visit Portland's historic Old Port district along Portland Harbor, at the mouth of the Fore River and part of Casco Bay. Portland Head Light is located in nearby Cape Elizabeth and marks the entrance to Portland Harbor. While on the I-295 at 60 miles an hour, it is good time to admiring the skyline.

No trip to Maine is complete without getting away from it, and on the breathtaking ocean waters and surrounding islands. Travelers who stop in Portland are also rewarded with ferries to offshore islands, boutique shops, historic homes, architectural treasures, graceful neighborhoods -- and top-shelf dining. Portland is the culinary mecca of northern New England, blessed with an uncommonly number of top-shelf restaurants for a city its size. With about 230 restaurants, Portland is believed to have the most restaurants per capita of any city in the United States. It is worth to plan a vacation for a weekend stay.

Victoria Mansion is a recognized as one of the finest Italianate style larkmark that designed by Henry Austin, a New Haven architect. It is known as the Morse-Libby House but rarely called that. It was built between 1858 and 1863 for a Maine businessman who had made his fortune in hotels in New York, Boston and New Orleans. Ruggles Morse died in 1893 and house and its contents were sold to Joseph Ralph Libby, a Portland merchant. Libby's family moved out in 1928 and planned to demolish it. However, it was saved by William H. Holmes who bought the house in order to preserve it and opened as a museum in 1941. Special events and extra tours usually occur in weeks before Christmas

Eagle Island was summer home of Robert E. Peary, a famed Arctic explore, who claimed in 1909 to be the first person to reach the North Pole. After Peary's death in 1920, his family continued to live in the house. In 1955, his family donated it to the state of Maine. Now Maine manages it as a state park and open to the public. The way to get to here is by taking your own boat or a charter boat.

Visit Convention and Visitor's Bureau of Greater Portland for more information about Portland 's attractions. Large supply of brochure are provided and staffs are happy to dispense information about local attractions, lodging, and dining. Portland also has a free weekly newspaper, Portland Phoenix, offering good listings of local events, films, and nightclub performances. Copies are widely available at restaurants, bars, and convenience stores.
