Philadelphia Introduction

Philadelphia is one of the most historic cities with greatest concentration of historic sites in America. Philadelphia attracts three million visitors to the city every year. It ideal place to spend a long weekend. Philadelphia known as a good place to balance work and family life. Be sure to see the beloved Liberty Bell and Independence Hall in the Independence National Historic Park. Independence Hall is where the Declaration of Independence and Constitution were signed. Other sites worth a visit are Franklin Court and Christ Church, where George Washington worshipped.

While you are walking around the historic area, you may encounter Philadelphia's most famous citizen, Benjamin Franklin. Philadelphia has some outstanding art museums, including the Perelman Building, Barnes Foundation, Woodmere Art Museum, Philadelphia Museum of Art, Rodin Museum, and Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts.

Philadelphia has been in the forefront of the nation's economic, intellectual, and humanitarian development but still preserving the best of the dignified past while allowing for the development of a vigorous new city. The central part of the city is also probably one of the most suitable for a stroll in the neighborhoods with trees, parks, and open spaces. So wear your comfortable shoes, arrive by public transportation and explore the nation's 5th largest city on foot.

Philadelphia offers many attractions suitable for families with children, including the Please Touch Museum, The Academy of Natural Sciences, Franklin Institute Science Museum, Philadelphia Zoo, Adventure Aquarium, The Bazemore Gallery, Rodin Museum, Muter Museum, and Sesame Place. After all that sightseeing, you'll be hungry, so head over to Reading Terminal Market, where you can sample a variety of local and ethnic foods, including Pennsylvania Dutch specialties, or take a walk through the Italian Market, located the famous South 9th Street. There are some recommended eateries for a little taste of Italian dishes, including Butcher's Block Cafe, Ralph's Italian Restaurant, Georges Sandwich Shop, Lorenzo's Pizza, and Villa di Roma Restaurant. While you're in Philadlephia, try Cuban and Caribbean cuisine at Alma de Cuba. Don't forget to try a soft pretzel with mustard and a Philly cheesesteak.
