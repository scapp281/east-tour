Concord Introduction

Concord is the capital city of New Hampshire. Today, the city is a center for health care and several insurance companies. Concord is within the humid continental climate zone. It has the long, cold, snowy winter, but it also has the very humid summers, autumns and springs. The highest temperature in Concord is 102 degree.

Concord is home to Granite State Symphony Orchestra. The Granite State Symphony Orchestra brings live classical symphonic music to people of all ages and economic means The GSSO has brought the symphony not only to the general public, but also to children of different ages in preschool and school and seniors who aren't able to travel. Audiences are pleased with the quality of this orchestra. It is also home to Concord Litho, one of the largest independently owned commercial printing companies in the United States.

One of the attractions is McAuliffe-Shepard Discovery Center, a science museum dedicated to Christa McAuliffe, who died in the 1986 Challenger space-shuttle explosion. Christa McAuliffe, a social studies teacher from Concord High School, who was selected by NASA out of 11000 applicants to be participated in Teacher in Space Project. Teacher in Space Project is a NASA program announced by Ronald Reagan in 1984 designed to inspire students, honor teachers, and spur interest in mathematics, science, and space exploration. This museum is also commemorates Alan Shepard, first American astronaut to travel into space in 1961 for 16 minutes. Throughout the week, there are astronomy shows about astronomy, aviation, earth and space science. These shows are tailored to different interests and age groups. There is also an observatory with telescope to look at details in the night sky. At the gift shop, there are astronomy-related trinkets that you can buy for friends and relatives.

Museum of New Hampshire History, founded in 1823, offers exhibitions on the state's heritage and traditions. Also, a variety of programs for children and adults, including school tours, outreach programs to classrooms across the state, workshops, lectures, demonstrations, courses, family days, and technical workshops. For example, the museum's Hands-on History Family Room offers activities that make learning fun for children. In this museum, there are collections of objects and archives to help storytelling New Hampshire's history to each generation. These collections are collected and preserved from the general public.
