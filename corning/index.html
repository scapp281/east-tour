Corning Introduction

Corning is known as the Crystal City. Corning was named after Erastus Corning, an Albany financier and railroad executive who was invest in developing the city. Although the city is famous for its glass industry, lumber was the first real industry of the region. The first settlers used the area's river systems to transport logs and finished lumber in fleets downstream to buyers.

Some of attractions nearby Corning are Spencer Crest Nature Center, Corning Museum of Glass, Benjamin Patterson Inn Museum Complex, Rockwell Museum of Western Art, Painted Post Depot Museum, and Watkins Glen State Park. Many of the cultural events and historic landmarks in the city are in Corning's Gaffer District.

In Corning, you can visit the popular local spots--Benjamin Patterson Inn Museum Complex, Corning Museum of Glass and the Rockwell Museum of Western Art. Spend your leisure hours at Denison Park, Stewart Park or Meadows Park and enjoy the nature. Bloody Run is the site of a battle between the Americans forces and Native American Villagers. The city also offers several shopping centers such as Steuben Glass Shop Center-Way, Van Heusen Factory Outlet, and Bass Shoe Outlet.

Corning has been cited several times by American Style magazine as one of the top 25 city for arts destinations. Corning Museum of Glass houses the world's most comprehensive collections of glass objects from ancient to present. Inside the museum is the Rakow Library, one of the world's major glass research centers. Other major cultural attraction is the Rockwell Museum of Western Art. In this museum, you will see an important collection of Western American painting and sculpture assembled over the past 40 years by Robert F. and Hertha Rockwell.
