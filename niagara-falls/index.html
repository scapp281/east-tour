Niagara Falls Introduction

Niagara Falls is a city in Niagara County, New York, United States. It is across the Niagara River from the city of Niagara Falls, Ontario. Both cities are named after the Niagara Falls, a major center of international tourism, which they share. The area was dominated by the Neutral Nation of Native Americans before Europeans entered the area. The first known European to see the falls is Robert de la Salle. European migrated into the area in the 17th century, which open warfare in competition for fur trade.

The City of Niagara Falls was incorporated on March 17, 1892 from the villages of Manchester and Suspension Bridge, which were parts of the Town of Niagara. New York State Governor Roswell P. Flower signed a bill into law forming the city. Thomas Vincent Welch who was a member of the charter committee and then a New York state assemblyman, but more importantly a second-generation Irishman, was there when the bill was signed, and responsible for asking Governor Flower to sign the bill on St. Patrick's Day. George W. Wright was elected the first mayor of Niagara Falls. The city was built around factories that utilized the power of the falling water for energy. During the Industrial Revolution, the city was a heavy industrial area due to the huge power offered by the Niagara River. There were many industries in Niagara Falls that used the power generated by the massive waterfall.

Go up the Prospect Point Observation Tower by elevator for a vista of panoramic view of all three Niagara Falls, including the American Falls. You can see side views of the American Falls and portion of the Bridal Veil Falls. The tower is The tower was originally built in 1961 with constructed of aluminum, glass, and steel.
