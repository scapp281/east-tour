Miami Introduction

Miami is dubbed by Forbes Magazine as a “America’s Cleanest City” in 2008. The city has another nickname called "The Magic City", a feat for a city that consisted of nothing but wilderness to the 42nd most populous city in the US. Since the late 1980, Miami transformed its image and merged as a center of international finance and commerce. The city plays a large role in the media industries, commerce, entertainment, transportation, finance, and media industries. In 2010, Miami ranked seventh in the United States in terms of culture, fashion, education, entertainment, and other sectors. Miami 's skyline is the 18th best in the world.

Miami's average monthly temperature has never been below 64. Miami has turquoise waters, white-sand beaches with its subtropical , sunny climate, which is perfect for sunbathing in spectacular beaches. Key Biscayne is known for its beaches. Miami has been a haven for tourist and retirees. Miami offers a diversity of lifestyles and attractions to both residents and visitors in a variety of small towns and cities such as Coral Gables, Hialeah, Miami Beach, Bal Harbor, South Beach, and Coconut Grove. If you don't like beaches, Crandon Park offers activities such as windsurfing, sail boarding, jet skiing, biking, picnicking, and partying.

Miami divides into three districts-- Downtown, North, West and South. Downtown (Central Business District, Park West, Omni, Brickell) is home to theaters, shops, company headquarters, courthouses, banks, and many historic buildings. North including Midtown, Overtown, Design District, Little Haiti, and Upper Eastside. Midtown is the fast growing community in Miami. Design District is a creative neighborhood and shopping destination. This place is an eye opener in fashion, design, art, and architecture. Little Haiti is a neighborhood where you could see Haitian culture. Upper Eastside is primarily a residential neighborhood, where you could see historic single family homes from the 1920s.
