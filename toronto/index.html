Toronto Introduction

Toronto, Canada's largest city and a metropolis of 5.7 million. Toronto is North America's fourth largest city with most vibrant that made up of the influence from over 200 ethnicities. thanks to the waves of young immigrants, who have transformed a rather sober destination into the world’s most cosmopolitan. Toronto is a good place for shopping, dining, and exploring attraction such as the Royal Ontario Museum, CN Tower, Toronto Island, and Art Gallery of Ontario.

The best place to start off the Toronto trip is at the top. There is no mistaking where that is. The landmark CN Tower is a 553 meter high communications and observation tower situated close to Lake Ontario. Take the elevator to the top for a breathtaking view of the city and its surrounding areas. With that view accompanies you and your lover for dinner is incredible.

You can work your way to explore the rest of Toronto from the CN Tower. Rogers Centre is next to CN Tower, where you can catch a baseball game of Blue Jays. Ideal activity for families and aquatic lover is the Ripley's Aquarium located at the base of CN Tower. If you are into art and history, Royal Ontario Museum and Art Gallery of Ontario are the places to go. Royal Ontario Museum has the fascinating archaeology and natural history exhibits. While Art Gallery of Ontario offer a fine collection of European and Canadian works.

One of the popular way to tour around Toronto is to hop on a streetcars named Red Rocket and visit Queen Street West. In this area, there are wide array of funky stores, galleries, ethnic restaurants, boutiques, and Easton Centre, which is one of the city's largest indoor shopping malls. Another place to visit is Distillery District, a historic and entertainment precinct located east of Downtown Toronto. It has numerous cafes, restaurants, shops, and Victoria-era architecture.

After a long way, a good place to rest is the beautiful beach opened in 2010. Sugar Beach is located across from Redpath Sugar Refinery. Or take a short ferry ride to Toronto Islands, as known as Menecing. Toronto Islands are chain of small islands and are a popular recreational destination for picnicking, cycling, rollerblading, or simply strolling. Bicycles, quadracycles, and canoes can be rented on the islands as well. The views of the city from Toronto Islands are stunning.

During the night, it is time to think where to go for dining, nightlife, and entertainment. Toronto has number of new or established restaurants to satisfy your appetite. Varies restaurant with cuisines from Canadian to Italian, Indian to fusion, burgers to bistros at restaurants such as Zocalo, Canoe, Vi Pei Cliffside, Porchetta, Drift, Allen's, Woodlot, Zoe's, Quinta Restaurant, Monkey Bar & Grill, Sidecar, Table 17, Indian Cuisine by the Lake, just to name a few. Watch a musical after dinner will be nice, would it? Shaw Festival or Stratford Theatre Festival offer fine performing arts. Shaw Festival is a major Canadian theatre festival, the second largest repertory theatre company in North America.
